## Why Dedicated Over VPS?

Real talk — a lot of sites don't need a dedicated server. If you're running a mid-traffic blog or a small SaaS, a VPS does the job fine. But once you hit a point where:

- You need **predictable, consistent compute** (no "noisy neighbor" dragging your CPU down)
- You're hosting **resource-intensive workloads** — game servers, transcoding, large databases, high-traffic e-commerce
- You need **compliance or data isolation** (your data, your physical machine)
- Or you're just tired of a VPS buckling under traffic spikes

…that's when a dedicated server stops being a luxury and starts being a requirement.

Anyhostlab is a newer, leaner provider. They're not sitting on 20 years of reviews and awards. The Dual Scalable Silver 4116 at $289/mo carries a "0–3 days deployment" note — suggesting it's not instant-deploy like the rest. Worth factoring in if you need something live quickly.

## The Bandwidth Math

Here's why "unmetered 1Gbps" actually matters: at 1Gbps sustained, you could theoretically transfer around **324TB per month**. Even at 10% average utilization — a realistic figure for a busy server — you're pushing 32TB/mo. At most hosts with capped plans, that's either a premium tier or an overage bill you don't want to see. Here, it's included at every price point.

For game servers, streaming nodes, CDN edge nodes, or high-traffic web apps, this is a meaningful differentiator.
